How To Buy A House in the UK and the Role of Property Review Platform

  • 5 Jul 2021
  • By Kijii

Buying a new property in the UK can be overwhelming, especially for new homeowners. However, you can streamline the process. What factor should you consider, and how can a property review platform help? In this article, we will explore a guide on how to buy a home in the UK and the role property review platforms like Kijii play in helping you get your ideal home!

1. Ensure You Have the Required Finances Needed

Buying a home of your choice might require that you get a mortgage. However, before you can be granted a mortgage, you need to have 5% of the total sum of money required to buy the property. For example, if you intend to get a house worth 200,000 pounds, you will need to deposit a minimum of 10,000 pounds to get a mortgage. 

Therefore, it will be advisable to start getting your finances in order. You can do so by saving up the required amount needed for the deposit. While saving up this amount, make sure your credit score is good enough to get a mortgage. If you've got a bad credit score, it could hinder your chances of getting a loan from lenders. 

2. Get to Know the Amount You Can Borrow

Mortgage loans are taken by individuals who want to buy property. The mortgage loan taken has to be of the same value as the property you intend to buy. The repayment term for a mortgage loan can be up to 25 years. But this is dependent on the mortgage provider you intend to use. To know the amount of loan you are eligible for, you can use the nationwide mortgage calculator to check. So with this calculator, you will be able to know the amount of mortgage loan you can take. 

3. Hire a Property Review Service

When you want to buy a house, it is essential to know the type of home you want and the ideal area. It is a vital aspect that ensures that the homeowner gets the best value for their money. Here you will need to enlist a Property review service. Property review platforms help you find viewpoints on the property you want to buy. The only people who can tell if a house is suitable are those that have lived in it before. A Property review platform like Kijii helps you get resident reviews from various properties in the UK. The goal of enlisting the services of property review platforms like Kijii is to ensure that you're getting your money's worth on any property you want to buy. 

4. Go For a Property Within Your Budget and Requirements

Some areas have costly homes in the UK. If the cost of houses within that region falls within your budget, you can begin your search in those areas. Also, the size of the house you want to buy matters.

The goal here is to get a home that suits your standard and preferences. There might be a possibility that you won't get your desired home type in an area you like. You might see your house preference in a location that isn't your fancy. In such cases, you will need to think carefully before deciding. 

You should also consider the proximity to your work areas and the availability of nearby schools for the kids. If you find out that the property you have the desire to acquire is more expensive than your budget, you can save more till you get the right amount to acquire that home, or you settle for something within your budget. Another alternative is getting a mortgage provider to give you the desired amount you need for the house.

5. Learn About Various Mortgages

Before applying for a mortgage, it is essential to conduct extensive research on various mortgages. It is a vital aspect you should prioritize before choosing your desired home. Therefore, make sure an excellent monetary plan is in place. 

6. Have a Decision in Principle

The Decision in Principle (DIP) is a form of certification issued by a mortgage provider. This certification is a written agreement stating that the mortgage lender is willing to loan you the amount you require to buy your house. The validity of this certification is 30 days. 

However, this differs from one mortgage lender to another. Most real estate companies will ask for your DIP whenever they want to show you a property. Having a DIP assures them of your seriousness in getting a mortgage loan. It will also enable the real estate agent and the homebuyer to complete the sales easily.

7. Choose the Right Realtor

In a real sense, individuals can buy a home without assistance from a real estate agent. However, if you are buying for the first time, you need a realtor. One thing is sure: Everyone needs professional assistance regarding buying a property, and realtors help with that!

However, it would be ideal for you to choose the right realtor. There are cases where most individuals have to ask for recommendations from their friends and loved ones. Also, you can get a realtor from online directories. However, when you find a prospective realtor, get to know their references. 

One of the best ways to do that is by getting reviews from past clients. Our property review services at Kijii can help you with that. Kijii doesn't just help you get reviews on the house you want to purchase. We also help get reviews on the realtor in charge of brokering the deal. 

Whenever you are enlisting the services of a realtor, be sure their interests are the same as yours. It will help ensure that a successful deal is being brokered between the buyer and seller of the property. So if you want a good home for an affordable price, wait till the realtor comes up with an option that suits your preference. 

However, a deal that is closed quicker is most beneficial for realtors. However, many realtors are more ethical and diligent when carrying out their job. Consult with those ones. 

8. Begin Your Search

This is where the real deal starts. When searching for your new home, use property websites to make the search easier. You'll come across so many property listings, and you will be able to choose from the many available options.

However, it would also be great if you also carried out a physical inspection of the apartment of your choice. Looking at the photos is excellent, but a physical visit to the property is best. When checking the property, feel free to forward any puzzling question to your realtor for a precise answer. 

9. Make Your Offer on the Property

Once you have narrowed down your search to your ideal choice, it's time to make an offer on your choice of property. Here you will make a decision on how much you are willing to offer. The number of people making offers for the house you intend to buy also plays a significant role. 

It would be ideal for you to find out the amount of other similar properties around. If the amount of other similar properties is below the asking price, make a lower offer. There are instances where your agent might tell you to offer the highest price you intend to pay for the property. However, this might not be a great idea as it won't give you the much-needed advantage during the negotiation process.

10. Offer Accepted. What Does This Mean?

When your offer is accepted, it doesn't mean that the sale is completed. There are no legally binding documents yet. Therefore, the seller can still choose to back out of the deal if contracts between both parties are not yet exchanged. You can talk to the seller to take the property out of the market since you are close to acquiring it. However, you should know that there is no obligation to hold the seller. As long as there is no binding document between the seller and buyer, the seller can choose to back out.  

11. Apply for a Mortgage and Begin the Property Valuation

At this time, you must have made your pick from the available mortgage providers. Now you are sure of your mortgage option. The mortgage lender will make available a check that will be displayed on your credit report. Should your application get turned down by the lender, you should find out why instead of immediately applying for another mortgage option. Making wrong conclusions can affect your credit score, so you need to be sure before going for another mortgage option. 

Once your mortgage application has been approved, the lender conducts a property valuation to determine if the property is worth the amount you are willing to pay. The lender also checks for issues with the property to see if any red flags could lead to extra costs. Once the valuation is completed, you can begin talks with the seller. 

Here, you can negotiate a lower price if you feel that necessary repairs need to be done on the property. Formal offers for mortgage loans are approved within four weeks of your application. The offer validity is for six months. Also, you should note that most mortgage providers want home insurance for your house before lending. So you might want to have home insurance before applying for your mortgage loan.

12. Get a Home Insurance

Home insurance is a condition given by most mortgage providers. Once an exchange has been completed between you and the seller, you are legally the owner of the property. So if anything catastrophic happens to the building after the exchange without home insurance, you won't be covered. 

13. Exchange of the Contracts

At this point, the sale becomes bound legally. The seller's solicitor and yours will carry out an exchange of the signed contracts. After this, you make the payment to complete the process.

14. Completing the Process and Moving in

The completion takes place two weeks after the contract exchange. At this stage, the money has already been successfully paid to the seller and the keys transferred to the buyer's custody. The property now belongs to you. 

You can now start carrying out maintenance work on the various areas necessary for your new home. Then you begin the fun aspect of decorating and furnishing your home according to your preference. Take a moment to reflect on your win and success and enjoy your new home.

Final Thoughts

When you plan to buy a home, the process can be quite tedious and lengthy. But with these tips we’ve discussed, you can have a smooth ride. Get to know the processes listed in this article before going for a home purchase!
