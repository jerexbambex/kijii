What To Look Out for When Renting a Property in the UK

  • 5 Jul 2021
  • By Kijii

Whether you're moving into an apartment for the first time or making a second move, renting an apartment is an exciting endeavour. However, finding a good apartment comes with many challenges, and no matter how versed you are in apartment renting, the process can be overwhelming. But with the right information, apartment hunting can become an enjoyable process. In this article, we’ll outline what to look out for when renting a property in the UK!

Pros and Cons of Renting 

Just like everything else, renting an apartment comes with its challenges. While there are many good aspects of property renting in the UK, there are some drawbacks. Nevertheless, the first and most reliable step is to use an excellent property review site like Kijii. 

With this hint, let’s explore the pros and cons of renting a property below:

Advantages of Renting 

  • The primary responsibility for maintaining the property falls on the landlord. If there is a fault, all you have to do is inform the landlord, and it will be fixed. 
  • There is a lot of flexibility. For example, if you are using a monthly lease option, you can decide to move out within a twinkling of an eye. With the flexibility, you're not tied down to a location.
  • In most cases, renting an apartment is a cheaper option than buying. Websites like Kijii show a list of all the apartments in significant areas in London to choose from.
  • If you move and travel frequently, you will benefit from renting an apartment as the burden of maintenance doesn't fall on you. 

Disadvantages of Renting 

  • The landlord has the freedom to increase your rent at any time.
  • The rules of the lease agreement may be restrictive. If the lease agreement doesn't allow pets, then you can't bring them.
  • Renting a property doesn't come with any tax benefits.
  • You may not be allowed to modify the house without your landlord's approval. 
  • If real estate value increases, there is a chance that your landlord may evict you. 
  • There is no opportunity for you to build your equity when you rent. So yes, the apartment will be your home, but it won't be an asset for you. 

Factors To Consider When Renting a Property in the Uk

It is estimated that over five million people in the Uk live in rented apartments. This has caused the government to set up rules protecting renters from vicious landlords. Also, review sites like Kijii allow renters to have all the information about a landlord before renting an apartment. 

Before renting an apartment, it is vital to know all the requirements. It can be frustrating to rent an apartment you thought you liked, only to move in and find many things you missed. The following points highlight some factors to consider when renting an apartment:

1. Use a Property Review Site 

Whether it is a no-pet policy, bad living conditions, awful landlord, or noisy neighbours, there are a lot you deserve to know before you commit your finances to rent. When you use Kijii, you can review the house you want to move in anywhere in London. For instance, if you want to move to an area in Manchester, you can search for that location and apartment using our website/app. All information you need concerning the living conditions of that apartment will be made available to you. So, you will not find out something horrible about the apartment after payment. 

2. Expenses 

The next thing you need to consider before hunting for an apartment is your budget. You need to check the monthly cost of the apartment and know if you can afford it. Imagine finding a great apartment only to realise that you can't afford it. So, it is best to check your finances before starting any process. You should avoid putting yourself through unwarranted disappointment and work out your finances. 

Your budget should include living costs, gas, internet, cable, and council tax. Some of these things can add to the expenses and make the cost of the apartment more expensive. Usually, old houses will cost more than modern apartments because of heating. A rule of thumb is to ensure that your rent apartment is under thirty percent of your monthly income. This would help you to save on your monthly expenses. 

3. Location 

Location is also an essential factor to consider before you rent an apartment. The area must be a preferred choice for you. Whatever location you choose will be a part of your everyday living. For example, your location will determine where you shop, where your kids will go to school, and your closeness to a hospital. All these are essential factors to consider when choosing a location. Also, verify the safety of the areas. 

A good property review site like Kijii is tailored for these situations. Property review sites will give you all the information you need concerning a particular location based on the feedback of people that have lived there. When armed with such information, you can easily decide which location suits you best without stepping foot in it. 

An apartment close to your place of work and kids' school will save you money on transportation. However, properties located near tube stations will cost more. But, they are beneficial in the long run. 

4. House Size 

After determining the apartment's location, the next factor to consider is the size of the apartment. If you live alone, you may not need a very large apartment. Remember, the bigger the apartment, the more expensive it will likely be. You must ask questions like, "Will the apartment has enough space for me and my kids?" "Does it have enough room to accommodate visitors?"

It is important to get honest answers to these questions before committing. Kijii is there to answer you because we have a list of major apartments in the UK listed on our site according to size and price. Of course, if you opt for a bigger apartment, you will pay more for utility. 

5. Pet-free or Not

Whether you are a pet owner or you plan to own pets in the future, you need to know if your potential apartment is a pet-free apartment. Pets could be noisy, so some apartments don't allow them. In extreme cases, pets can be dangerous to other tenants in an apartment. Ask whether pets are allowed in the apartment you plan to move to. If you use a property review site like Kijii, you will see a list of apartments that allow pets and those that don't. If pets are allowed, you should check if there are parks nearby where you can take your pet. This is especially important for dog owners. Do not sneak pets into an apartment where they are not allowed. If your landlord finds out you sneaked a pet, you stand a chance of being evicted. 

6. Viewing Process 

Sites like Kijii have pictures of potential houses you intend to rent. It also highlights the amenities in the houses. Some houses look good online but don't look great when you visit. On the other hand, some houses may not be so great online but excellent when you check them out in real life. However, with Kijii, what you see on the website is what you get! 

7. Deposit Protection 

The deposit you make on properties rented in the UK is protected. The tenancy deposit protection scheme protects the deposits. Your landlord is obligated to put your money in the scheme. They also tell you where they have kept the money within thirty days of you giving them the money. The tenancy deposit scheme aims to protect you and your interest. So, it is essential to ask your landlord about it before you move in. You should count it as a red flag if your landlord is dodgy about placing your deposit in the scheme. 

Tips on Getting Cheaper Rent for an Apartment 

It is one thing to get an apartment; it is another thing to get an apartment that won’t make you break the bank. With the rising cost of rent, one has to be resourceful in getting an apartment. You can negotiate lower rates, but it is an art and requires a lot of creative ability. Below are some steps to get cheaper rents on apartments in the UK:

1. Stay Informed About Your Local Market 

You can stay informed by using Kijii. With Kijii, you can type whatever your location is in London, and you will get the rent prices in your area. If the prices change, the website will update to the new price. When you are informed about the rent price, you will be in a better position to negotiate. It is a bigger advantage for you in the high vacancy rates in an area, and there are a lot of empty apartments. 

2. Know the Best Timing 

Timing is essential when you want to rent an apartment. If you are worried about the budget, winter is usually the best time to rent. This is because most people don't want to move when it's winter. In addition, many people prefer to stay indoors rather than go house hunting. So, if you want to get apartments at knock-off prices, schedule your move from December through to January. 

3. Check Out the Competition 

You can walk around the neighbourhood and ask neighbours about vacancies and prices. There is nothing wrong if you walk into lease offices and ask for their prices. After the official launch of a building, there is usually a lease-up offer. These lease-up offers mean that you can get cheaper rent during the first six months after a new building complex is launched. Some offers may include an application fee waiver or a month of free rent. The better you know, the more you increase your chances of getting a lower rent. 

4. Co-renting 

Co-renting is an easy way to cut costs. It also helps you to enjoy some company while you save money. Of course, before you co- rent, make sure you have a good relationship with your roommate. It is also good to set ground rules about who pays utilities and handles the rent. There should also be a stated rule about overnight guests. The situation will be more manageable if you're both determined to make the best of the arrangement.
